## World

**DragonLand** is a large island continent — the Dragonborn homeland and primary setting. The tavern (La Última Gota) sits at its geographic center, a crossroads for all traffic across the continent. Forests cover much of the land, with arid plains in the interior and mountains dividing north from south. A network of trade routes connects dozens of settlements.

**Dragon Born** is a smaller island off DragonLand's eastern coast — the fortress-capital of Dragonborn power. It houses the throne, the military headquarters (Dragonforce), castles, barracks, and an execution plaza. The population speaks Draconic.

**Brasboredon** is a fortified town in the north-central region of DragonLand. The party is currently here, fighting through a full-scale revolution.

**The Throne of DragonLand** is contested between two half-brothers:

- **Ala Rota** (a.k.a. Prince Roto / Robin) — the legitimate heir, now assassinated.
- **Dranlek "El Oscuro"** (a.k.a. La Iguana) — the bastard half-brother who seized the throne and is the **current king** of DragonLand.

After Prince Robin's assassination, Dranlek seized the throne at Dragon Born and established a dictatorial regime over DragonLand. **Dragonforce** — once the legitimate military serving dragonborn royalty — now enforces the new order.

### The Five Great Houses of DragonLand

DragonLand is governed by a feudal system of five noble houses, each associated with an element and ruling a domain:

| House | Element | Domain | Title |
|-------|---------|--------|-------|

Between the house domains lies the **Dragon Scar** — a no-man's-land also known as "Tierras de Lunei," where La Última Gota tavern is located.

## Prophecies

### The Grey Dream (El Sueño Gris)

**Source:** Lock and Hook (twin elves from Las Tierras Lándicas)
**First Mentioned:** Chapter 2

> *"After the death of the hybrid prince and the rise of the lying tongue, the true dragon shall come to the throne. And if the true dragon claims power, it will seek the destruction of the Tree of Life. And if the Tree of Life falls — the sleeping dragon awakens."*

The elves treat this as an ancient bedtime story — "like Santa Claus for elves." Lock and Hook left the Lándicas because of this prophecy. The party does not yet understand its full implications, but Prince Robin's assassination and La Iguana's rise to power may have already triggered the first conditions.

**Possible Connections:**
- "The death of the hybrid prince" → Prince Robin (Ala Rota)'s assassination
- "The rise of the lying tongue" → Dranlek "El Oscuro" (La Iguana) seizing the throne? Or the Veil of the Seven Songs placing agents on every throne?

- "The Tree of Life" → Unknown location/artifact
- "The sleeping dragon" → Unknown — a cataclysmic entity

## Historical Events

### The Assassination of Prince Robin / Ala Rota (Príncipe Roto)

**When:** Before the campaign
**Significance:** Destabilized the political order of all kingdoms

Prince Robin — also known as **Ala Rota** ("Broken Wing") — was a Dragonborn and the legitimate heir to the DragonLand throne. He was the great-great-grandson of the original dragon who fused his bloodline with humanity. Massive (~2.50m) with large wings (lost one fighting the Crystal Elemental Dragon). The only Dragonborn in the bloodline who emerged victorious from great battles. His **assassination** — killer never found — upended the political landscape. After his death, his half-brother **Dranlek "El Oscuro"** (the bastard heir, known derogatorily as "La Iguana") seized the throne and established a dictatorial regime.

The demon Victus was tasked by his god to "destroy the Fallen Prince" — this may refer to Robin or a connected figure.

**Secret:** Tidus killed Prince Roto, the heir to the Dragonborn throne.

### The Fall of the Southern Elven Tribe

**When:** ~90 years before the campaign (Tidus was 15)
**Significance:** Origin of Tidus's character

Tidus's people were destroyed — not by force, but by betrayal. Allies failed them. Half were killed. Tidus was moved north and taken in by another tribe that tolerated him but never fully trusted him.

### The Corruption of the Veil

**When:** More than 5 years before the campaign
**Significance:** Turned a benevolent order into a political weapon

Someone close to Keylan twisted the Veil of the Seven Songs from palliative care (easing suffering through music) into a weapon of political infiltration. The Veil now has agents on every throne. Nixira fled, was framed by Keylan, and has been in hiding for ~5 years.

## Key Artifacts & Items

### Corona Dorada (Golden Crown)

**Holder:** Malacor / Farquad
**Type:** Mind-control artifact

A single command compels absolute obedience. Malacor is using it to control people and destabilize existing power structures. The party and Dracus have allied to destroy it. Zacarías denies ever giving it to Malacor.

### Ghost Essence (Esencia Fantasmal)

**Type:** Cursed substance
**Effect:** Seeks the strongest spiritual presence and drags them into a deathly sleep

Used by the Bald Enemy at the tavern. Targeted Lunei Milei Maili because he had the strongest spiritual presence. Without his former paladin's holy ward, he had no defense. The cure requires a **ground emerald** — a rare mineral found at the river settlement or elven mining camps.

### The Arcane Library

**Location:** Hidden chamber inside the mountain pass caves
**Type:** Unknown magical repository

A room full of arcane books. When Zacarías's raven entered, a disembodied voice spoke: *"Read me, for there lies your power."* The raven was consumed/absorbed. The library's nature, contents, and the entity behind the voice are all unknown. Zacarías will need to return.

### Moonstone (Piedra Lunar)

**Holder:** Zacarías
**Value:** 10 platinum (100 gold)

Found by Zacarías's raven in a trapped room inside the mountain caves. The raven had to choose between the moonstone and a compass — it took the moonstone. The compass vanished, relocated by some hidden mechanism.

### Blood-Web Shield

**Holder:** Zacarías
**Source:** Malfas, Prince of Ravens
**Charges:** 16 remaining (originally 17)

A lattice of black webbing woven from Zacarías's own blood, granted by his patron after the raven's death. The cost: no more ritual casting for the session it was granted.

### The Forbidden Chi-Astral Book

**Stolen by:** Fuercitas (youngest monk pupil)
**Significance:** Got the entire Nameless Order group imprisoned on the Prison Tortuga

A book about connecting the "chi-astral" — forbidden knowledge within the Nameless Order. Fuercitas stole it, leading to the capture and imprisonment of Señor Nadie and all three pupils.

### List of 24 People

**Holder:** Nixira / Party
**Source:** Found in Lunei Milei Maili's pocket

Purpose unknown — possibly a hit list, debt list, witness list, or quest targets. Found during the chaos at the tavern. Significance not yet revealed.

### Dragon Whistles

**Holder:** Farquad's dragon trainers
**Effect:** Can summon/control trained dragons

May be able to control Line, the baby rock dragon tamed by Tidus.

## Magical Concepts

### Ghost Essence Curse

A cursed substance that manifests as living shadow/ink. Seeks the strongest spiritual presence in a room and drags them into a magical coma — an "oneiric dream state." The victim appears in a dark void, a black rippling swamp. Cure: ground emerald, administered before two quarter-moons pass.

### The "Don" (Musical Mind Manipulation)

The gift possessed by members of the Veil of the Seven Songs. Allows mental manipulation through music. Originally used for palliative care. Nixira possesses it but claims never to have used it on her companions. The extent of its power is unclear.

### Malfas's Patronage

Zacarías's warlock pact with Malfas, the Prince of Ravens. Grants a raven familiar, illusion magic, and emergency protections (blood-web shield). The relationship is transactional and volatile — Malfas answers calls with contempt and charges steep prices. The raven familiar has been destroyed twice (arcane library, acid).

### Condemned Spirits

Spirits trapped in the volcanic lake beneath the demon's lair. Awakened by Nixira's Thaumaturgy, they spoke to Victus — and through them, his god's voice reached him. Their nature and why they are condemned is unknown.
