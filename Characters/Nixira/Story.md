# Nixira Silverson — Story Arc

| Detail | Info |
|--------|------|
| **Real Name** | Kenitzira |
| **Stage Name** | Mai Melody / Melo |
| **Race** | Tiefling ("my lineage doesn't control me") |
| **Class** | Bard (Cornetist / Musician) with combat proficiency (crossbow, daggers) |
| **Tavern Role** | Cook and House band leader at La Última Gota |

**Personality:** Emotional but tough. Fiery — literally has fire in her eyes when provoked, and small horns emerge when angered. Dramatic and proud with strong stage presence. Deeply loyal to friends. Competitive (bitter rivalry with Rainbow). Hot-headed and honor-obsessed — challenges others to dragon chess when offended. Stubborn but forgiving, genuinely moved by apologies. Self-deprecating about her cooking versus her music. Creative in combat — uses multiple voices and psychological warfare through music. Carries deep guilt and fear about her past. Brave enough to reveal her secret history to the group.

**Secret-Keeper:** **Rainbow** held Nixira's secret. Rainbow's current status is **uncertain** — DM planning notes suggest he is dead, but session transcripts show him escaping the tavern alive. If dead, Nixira's secret may have died with him. If alive, Rainbow's bitter rivalry with Nixira makes him especially dangerous as a secret-holder.

**Backstory:** A famous cornetist not heard publicly in over 5 years. Former member of the **Veil of the Seven Songs** — a secret society of musical witches that originally used mental manipulation through music for palliative care (easing suffering of the magically ill). The organization was corrupted by someone close to **Keylan**, who weaponized their powers to infiltrate the politics of every kingdom, placing agents on every throne. Nixira fled when she discovered the corruption. **Keylan betrayed and framed her**, and the Veil is hunting her. She hid at the tavern for ~2 years, working in the kitchen where no one sees her. Her identity was exposed during a performance. Possibly has a twin sister.

**Abilities:**
- Thaumaturgy (tiefling racial — voice amplification, multiple echoing voices, floating flames)
- Minor Illusion
- Firebolt cantrip
- Bardic Inspiration
- Vicious Mockery / vocal attacks ("vociferación")
- "Bloom Boys" (magical voice amplification — 3x volume for 1 minute)
- Persuasion +5, Deception +5
- Mental manipulation through music (the "don" from the Veil — extent of power unclear)
- Crossbow combat, dagger combat
- Instruments: harp, cornet, mini percussion
- Dragon chess challenges (honor-based conflict resolution)

## Chapter 1 — The Tavern

Prepared poisoned "special orders" using centipede essence in the kitchen. Announced on stage, triggering Rainbow's fury (his companions had to physically restrain him). Locked eyes with Rainbow defiantly: *"You'll have the honor of hearing real musicians tonight."* Orchestrated a "salon dance" using Bloom Boys to clear the room, giving Lunei's crossbows clean firing lines. Shifted music tempo to de-escalate violence.

In the combat aftermath, used Thaumaturgy to capture enemy attention. Organized musical march to surround enemies. Cast Firebolt and threw daggers. Revealed her real identity as Kenitzira. Felt she could no longer stay at the tavern. Found a list of **24 people** in Lunei Milei Maili's pocket.

## Chapter 2 — The Plateau

Used Thaumaturgy to create floating flame (distracted serpent). Tried Minor Illusion to fake serpent voice — backfired because serpents are solitary; got bitten. **Serpent venom: ~22 hours until anaphylactic reaction.** Used Persuasion to make Zacarías feel genuine guilt ("the Grinch moment"). Revealed her full backstory as a former Veil of the Seven Songs member to the party. Shot a serpent with crossbow in crystalline water to retrieve a golden key. Wore a decapitated serpent as a belt. Helped pull a lever to raise an iron gate.

## Chapter 3 — The Flying Turtle

Struggled to break out of her cell. Fought **two mimics** — incinerated one with Firebolt, escaped the other by tickling its tongue-choking attack. Retrieved keys from mimics. Freed monk pupils. Witnessed Rodrigo's death (darts through the neck) and vowed to write a song for him. Confronted Branig the Double Edge (undead guard) at the main gate.

In the demon's lair, played calming harp to give Tidus advantage on charisma checks. Switched to war drums for combat. Used Thaumaturgy with multiple echoing voices to psychologically torment the demon, awakening **condemned spirits** — including the voice of his god. Kicked the demon for 1 damage. **Delivered the killing blow** with a crossbow bolt through the demon's skull, dedicating it to Zacarías: *"He may be an idiot, but he's MY idiot."*

## Chapter 4 — The Battle of Brasboredon

Stormed a watchtower during the revolution. Put tower guards to sleep with Command, executed them, and killed a regenerating green dragon. From the tower, fired the **fire ballistae** — releasing all remaining barrels at once, incinerating an enemy contingent below. The collateral damage killed dwarven children in the blast zone.

A **dwarf mother** with burned hands branded Nixira's face with a magical dagger, tattooing the **"asesina de niños"** rune on her left cheek — a permanent dwarven curse marking her as a child-killer. Any dwarf who sees it will despise her. Nixira cast Command: Mercy on the dwarf and let her leave with her children.

Rescued **Robinson** after he was knocked unconscious in the tower fighting. Led rebels in the streets. Performed a **Vicious Mockery freestyle rap battle** with Robinson — Nixira providing psychic-damage insults while Robinson beatboxed. Killed three draconid soldiers through sheer vocal devastation. Remembered **Lili** (her wife/love) in the aftermath.

Witnessed the orc hero's sacrifice at the gate mechanism. The party was separated by falling gates and gelatina barriers. Later seen **riding a Wyvern** near the Mansion — daggers buried in its eyes, steering it by pain to crash into two other attacking wyverns. Fighting alongside Bixira in aerial combat.

## Chapter 5 — The Escape from Brasboredon

Regained consciousness inside the walls after the failed explosion. Robinson presumed dead beyond the city walls, lost in the blast. Located Zacarías through a stone grate separating their sections. Used **Vociferación** to shout across: *"¡Zacarías! ¿Cómo estás?"* — reassured him: *"No te preocupes, Zacarías, te aceptaremos tal y como eres, un Warlock."*

Used **Mage Hand** to help operate the portcullis mechanism — first attempt failed (even a nat 20 wasn't enough alone), but combined with Zacarías pulling from the tower roof, they raised the gate together.

Navigated the second gate by crawling underneath it — digging through loosened earth beneath the base, barely fitting through. Emerged next to the Troll on Zacarías's side.

Climbed the **Mansion exterior** hand over hand up ivy to the roof. Found **Bixira** barely alive — half burned, fourteen arrows in her back, standing through sheer refusal to die. The General circled above on his dragon.

Witnessed Bixira's sacrifice: she launched a beam of pure white light toward the heavens, colliding with the General's volcanic meteorite. The explosion opened an **interdimensional portal to the Plano Oscuro** — stable for approximately five turns.

Fought a **Dragon Trainer** on the Mansion rooftop — a tall draconid chain-fighter and Bixira's former sparring partner. Pinned his chain with **Mage Hand**. Cast **Dissonant Whispers** — a note pitched to rupture his eardrums, leaving him completely deafened. Swept his legs, drove a dagger into his neck (6 damage). He kicked her back and partially freed his chain. Taunted him in sign language since he couldn't hear: *"Deberíamos besarnos para romper la tensión."*

The trainer spit acid-venom — hit before she could dodge. Drank a potion simultaneously, skin dissolving and regenerating at once. Tried to throw him off the roof; he barely held on.
