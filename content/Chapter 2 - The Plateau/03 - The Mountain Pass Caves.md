# Chapter 2.3 — The Mountain Pass Caves

**"They danced through serpents and darkness, only to find the real fangs waiting at the light."**

## Setting the Scene

The party has left the scorching, eternally-lit crater behind and arrived at the only southward passage through the mountains — a vast network of natural caverns expanded and fortified by the forest elves on the far side. Water seeps through the rock overhead from a cascading waterfall, torches line the damp corridors, and everywhere the tunnels echo with the slithering of serpents. The elven guard at the entrance lets them through, warns them not to damage the support structures (lest the whole passage flood), and then seals the iron gate behind them with a heavy clang. One detail catches Tidus's ear: someone recently fled through here in a panic, babbling about deaths and racing to carry intelligence to Dragonball. A sentinel. One of La Blanca's operatives. If that message reaches the wrong hands, Tidus knows he's a marked man.

The mood is tense. Lunei Milei Maili is dying in a magical coma somewhere behind them, serpents infest every shadow ahead, and the party can barely stand each other. The DM introduces a group irritation tracker — a meter that climbs with every insult, every reckless decision. Nixira and Zacarías are already at level two. At six, fists fly.

## The Story

Tidus steadies the group with a memory. He's been through these caves before — years ago, with a beautiful elf at his side, on a journey quiet enough to feel like a dream. He taught her a technique he calls the survival dance: a rhythmic, silent movement that produces no vibrations, no sound, nothing to alert the serpents coiled in every crevice. It's absurd. It's canonical. Everyone must physically dance for the duration of the session or risk a bite.

Nixira, never one to let a moment go unscored, begins singing a serpent ballad — something about a snake from the hot lands who shows its teeth when it laughs. Zacarías, meanwhile, puts his real talent to work: he casts Speak with Animals and interrogates a terrified rat. The news is grim. The caves are crawling with serpents hiding behind rocks and inside rubble, invisible until you step on them. Most of the rats are already fleeing. Only two or three remain.

Three hours into the tunnels — three hours of dancing through torch-lit darkness — they spot figures huddled in the gloom. Two elves, one impossibly white, the other impossibly black, pressed together against the cold. They are Lock and Hook, twin spice merchants from Las Tierras Lándicas — the mystical elven homeland where no one ages and existence drifts like an endless dream. They've been in the real world barely a year. They don't understand fear. They don't understand deception. They simply *are*. They're also Lunei Milei Maili's herb suppliers, carrying a signed invoice for five gold pieces of rare spices and looking for their business partner "Don Fernando." Tidus, ever the pragmatist, tries to shake them down for an unpaid tavern bill. The twins blink serenely and produce the receipt.

As the group walks — dances — together, the twins share something that lodges like a splinter in the mind. They left the Lándicas because of the Grey Dream, an ancient elven prophecy: *After the death of the hybrid prince and the rise of the lying tongue, the true dragon shall come to the throne. And if the true dragon claims power, it will seek the destruction of the Tree of Life. And if the Tree of Life falls — the sleeping dragon awakens.* They say it like it's a bedtime story. Like Santa Claus for elves. No one laughs.

The serpent encounters escalate. Zacarías, speaking Pársel, tries to negotiate with a snake, offering it a future meal in exchange for cooperation. The serpent constricts his arm until it goes numb, then refuses the deal. Nixira is bitten — the venom isn't neurotoxic but triggers a slow anaphylactic reaction. A twenty-four-hour countdown begins. Every hour matters now.

They reach a sealed door — a massive stone mechanism that requires the vibration of a rattlesnake to open. Zacarías's first plan is elegant: convince a serpent to enter the mechanism willingly. His second plan — using Minor Illusion to fake the voice of an elder serpent commanding obedience — backfires spectacularly. Serpents are solitary predators. They don't have hierarchies. They don't take orders. The snake recognizes the trick instantly and attacks. In the chaos, the serpent ends up forced into the mechanism. It is crushed. Blood flows through ancient channels and the door groans open — an elven blood sacrifice, a forbidden spell. The party exchanges glances. Whatever these caves are protecting, it's worth killing for.

Beyond the door they find a pool of crystal-clear water and a serpent coiled around a golden key. Nixira puts a crossbow bolt through the snake's head with surgical precision. They fill their waterskins, pocket the key, and Nixira wraps the decapitated serpent around her waist like a belt because, in her words, why not.

Then comes the emotional gut-punch. Nixira turns to Zacarías — who has spent the session scheming, lying, and shoving innocent elves toward danger — and simply looks at him. Disappointed. Hurt. She rolls a twenty on persuasion. The DM describes it like the Grinch discovering Christmas: Zacarías's heart swells three sizes. For one shining moment, the chaotic little gnome genuinely cares. "You were always my friends," he whispers, and means it. The effect is mechanical too — Nixira gains advantage on all rolls involving Zacarías for the rest of the session.

The golden key opens a gate. Lock, the white twin, touches it first — and disintegrates. Hook screams. Zacarías, without missing a beat, explains it was "an ancient teleportation spell" and rolls high enough on deception that the traumatized Hook believes him. Just barely.

What follows is a gauntlet: a trap room of pressure plates triggering fire cannons, arrow storms, rising spikes, and swinging blades. Zacarías sends his raven through. The bird finds two treasures — a moonstone worth ten platinum on the right, a compass on the left. It can only carry one. The raven snatches the moonstone. The compass vanishes, relocated by some hidden mechanism to somewhere else entirely.

Then the cave reveals its true secret. A mechanism drops an iron portcullis, fire erupts, and from the smoke emerges a wyrmling — a baby rock dragon, blind, navigating by sound and smell alone. The party freezes. Tidus does not. Still dancing, making no sound, he approaches the creature. He places his hand on its nose. The dragon goes still. He feeds it a dead serpent. The wyrmling rolls over and presents its back. Tidus mounts it and names it Line.

Exploring Line's lair, the party discovers this is no natural den. It's a nursery — constructed, equipped with training mechanisms, hatching infrastructure. Line is malnourished, scarred, undersized for his age. A fighting dog raised for someone else's war. Inside, Zacarías finds cave maps, thirty meters of climbing rope, and iron stakes. The exit is marked.

Deeper still, they find Dracus — the one-handed warlock, Tidus's old companion — pressed against a wall, terrified. The reunion is sharp-edged. Zacarías puts a blade to his throat. Dracus dematerializes it with a thought — Pact of the Blade — and smirks. Tidus steps between them. Dracus talks. He reveals that Malacor is using the Golden Crown to bend people to his will, that it's disrupting La Blanca's operations, and that Lunei Milei Maili had told him about a path to the Crown's destruction. Shared enemy. Reluctant alliance.

Then Nixira drops her own bomb. She confesses to the group: she was a member of the Veil of the Seven Songs, a secret society of musical witches who once eased suffering through mental manipulation — palliative magic for the incurably ill. Someone close to Keylan twisted the Veil's gift into a weapon, placing puppet agents on every throne in the known world. Nixira fled. Keylan framed her. The Veil hunts her still. She has the *don* — the gift of mental manipulation through music. The party stares. Dracus's hand drifts to his blade. Tidus asks the only question that matters: "When you taught me that song — did you do anything to me?" She swears she didn't. They believe her. For now.

Meanwhile, Zacarías's raven scouts a hidden chamber full of arcane books. A disembodied voice speaks: *"Read me, for there lies your power."* The raven is consumed — lost to whatever force dwells in that library. Zacarías staggers. His familiar is gone. But the voice echoes in his skull like a promise.

The party locates twenty-three people trapped deeper in the caves — most of them refugees from the tavern, stranded for over twenty-four hours because no guard has descended to lower the exit ropes. Using Line, the cave maps, and the climbing equipment, they begin the rescue.

Nixira scouts ahead. Her boot lands in something wet. Blood. She looks up just as a dart whistles past her face. At the cave mouth, silhouetted against daylight, sits Farquad on horseback. Behind him: soldiers, dragon trainers clutching whistles, and a Level 15 elven tactician who had been hiding among the twenty-three "refugees" below. Zacarías is unconscious. Tidus is restrained, Dracus clawing at the arms that hold him. Farquad leans forward in his saddle and smiles.

*"How long we've been looking for you."*

## Key Revelations

- **The Grey Dream prophecy** warns that a "true dragon" will seek the throne of Dragonball and destroy the Tree of Life, awakening a sleeping dragon — a cataclysm the Lándicas elves fled from.
- **The mountain pass caves conceal a dragon-training nursery**, complete with a captive wyrmling, training infrastructure, and heavy magical security (blood sacrifice locks, trap corridors). Someone is breeding dragons for war.
- **Nixira was a member of the Veil of the Seven Songs**, a secret society that mastered mental manipulation through music. She possesses the gift but claims never to have used it on her companions.
- **Zacarías's raven was absorbed by an arcane library** hidden in the caves. A voice promised power: "Read me, for there lies your power." The library remains unexplored.
- **Dracus confirms Malacor is wielding the Golden Crown** to control people and destabilize existing power structures.

---

## Consequences

- **Farquad has sprung a trap at the cave exit.** The party is being captured — Zacarías is unconscious, Tidus is restrained, and Farquad's forces include dragon trainers with whistles that may control Line.
- **Nixira's venom timer is ticking** — roughly twenty-two hours remain before anaphylactic shock from the serpent bite.
- **Nixira's identity as a former Veil operative is now known to the group**, fracturing trust at the worst possible moment.
- **Lock is gone** — disintegrated or teleported by the golden gate trap. Hook is alive but deeply shaken, his blind trust in strangers cracking.
- **The arcane library calls to Zacarías.** His raven is lost, but the promise of power remains. He will have to return.
- **Lunei Milei Maili is still dying.** Every hour spent in these caves is an hour closer to losing the boss — and their only lead on the ground emerald that could save him.
