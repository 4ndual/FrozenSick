# Chapter 4: The Battle of Brasboredon — Session 06
## *"The city burned from both sides, and the only currency left was blood."*

---

### Setting the Scene

The party has arrived at **Brasboredon**, a fortified town in the north-central region of DragonLand. Three days inside enemy territory. The city is tearing itself apart — a full-scale revolution rages through the streets. Rebel factions fight Dragonforce loyalists. Towers burn. Gates close. Gelatina barriers (living slime walls) seal off entire districts. Wyverns circle the skies above the Mansion. And somewhere in the chaos, three former tavern employees are trying to reach the heart of the city alive.

They carry communication stones — small magical pebbles that let them speak across distances. They'll need them. By the end of this day, they won't be in the same part of the city anymore.

---

### The Story

**Robinson Bridge — Tidus and the Ghost of DragonForce**

Tidus reaches the gates of Robinson Bridge and stops dead. On the bridge stands a dragon — totally black, thin, tall — casting dark green flames from both hands, engulfing the structure in eldritch fire.

The dragon turns. *"We meet again, Tidus."*

Tidus stares. He doesn't recognize the creature at first. Then the memories hit: a pathetic apprentice in DragonForce's castle, the butt of every joke, a would-be mage who couldn't produce a single spark. **Borax.** The runt who used to bring Tidus breakfast while trembling. The one Tidus once touched on the head and told *"I recommend you leave the castle"* — knowing what was coming.

But Borax has changed. The green fire tells the whole story. There are two ways to gain magical power in the draconic tradition: years of disciplined study, or a pact with something dark. Borax chose the second path. Around his neck hangs a **talisman** — a medallion already five-sixths full with stolen power, trading his soul for fire. If it fills completely, the entities that hold his contract will take his body.

Tidus sends his baby dragon, **Pikachu**, charging head-first into Borax. Critical hit — Borax staggers, enters frenzy, starts absorbing the wall of green flames to heal himself. The talisman pulses at five of six. Borax raises a hand, concentrating all his power into a single devastating attack.

Tidus shoots first. The crossbow bolt smashes into the center of the talisman. Eighteen points of damage, dead center. The medallion shatters.

What happens next is something half the city sees. All the fire in the area rushes toward Borax's body. His flesh expands, liquefies, melts — bones dissolving, skin pouring off like wax — until all that remains is a charred stone mass on the bridge and, rising from it, a towering **fire elemental**. A colossus of living flame, about to crush Tidus where he stands.

Tidus grabs his **Val medallion** — the symbol of the god of death — lifts it, and whispers: *"My moment has come. Remember me."* He closes his eyes.

A voice answers. Not his own. *"There's still time."*

His eyes snap open. One dagger, thrown with everything he has left. It strikes the charred mass of Borax's remains. The fire elemental shudders, collapses, and vanishes.

Tidus stands alone on the bridge. On his medallion, a rune appears — **1 of 20**. Val, god of death, accepts tributes. A rogue's assassination, done by mercy, is a noble kill. The counter has begun. What happens at 20 remains unknown.

---

**The Tower — Nixira's Firestorm**

While Tidus fought Borax, Nixira stormed a watchtower overlooking the gate district. She put the tower guards to sleep with a Command spell, executed them from behind, and killed a green dragon that was regenerating by sacrificing its own lifeforce. From the top of the tower, she surveys the carnage — battle spreading across the city, the Mansion engulfed in fireballs, reinforcements pouring from the barracks.

Two fire ballistae sit loaded on the tower's platform. Last barrels of ammunition. Nixira doesn't hesitate. She pulls every release lever at once, and the entire payload rains down on the enemy contingent below. A wall of fire swallows them.

But fire doesn't discriminate.

Nixira jumps from the tower and takes cover behind a group of dwarves with shields. An **enana** — a dwarf mother, hands burned, face streaked with tears — looks up at her and screams: *"Asesina! Asesina de niños!"* (Child killer!) She throws a dagger that grazes Nixira's face. The wound burns with magical fire. When it fades, a **rune** remains — tattooed into her left cheek. The dwarven curse of the child-killer. From now on, every dwarf who sees her will know what she did. Every dwarf who sees her will hate her.

Nixira stares at the enana. Remembers her own past. Redemption. Not wanting to be the monster.

*"Nena, no te voy a hacer daño. Vete."*

She casts Command: Mercy. The dwarf goes still. Her tears stop. She turns and walks away with her children. Nixira picks up Robinson — wounded, drifting in and out of consciousness — and keeps moving.

---

**The Gate — Zacarías's Reckoning**

Zacarías enters through the main gate alone, moving fast while the others fight. He summons his raven familiar and sends it soaring above the district, getting a bird's-eye view of the devastation. What it shows him breaks something inside.

Twenty children. Running. Their bodies melting in the fire. A dwarf woman on her knees, wailing, holding the charred bones of draconic children. All races. All dead.

Zacarías grabs his communication stone. *"Una guerra muerte contra los dragones,"* he snarls. Death war against the dragons. Use fire. Kill them all.

Tidus's voice crackles back: *"No es nada estúpido. Ni siquiera te importan los niños. Pero si te asquea, solo llega a la mansión."*

The words land. Zacarías stares at the bodies. He is a dragon — or at least, he wears the form of one. The racism he remembers from his past — against gnomes, against small races — is playing out in reverse. And he's about to become the thing he hated.

He climbs onto the rubble and speaks.

*"¿Cómo podemos ser diferentes? Todos podemos ser diferentes. No tenemos que armar una guerra y repetir la historia. Hay que tener compasión de los vivos — no de los muertos."*

I have a dream. The draconic prayer follows: *"Todos nacemos del fuego y morimos en el fuego."* All are born of fire and die in fire.

Ravens cry. The voice of his patron, Malfas, echoes through the stones. **One point of inspiration** — earned not through combat, but through conscience.

---

**The Gatehouse — Blood for the Bridge**

The three find each other again inside the city walls. They climb the gatehouse tower and discover a massacre on the stairs — seven bodies from a failed rebel assault — and three surviving draconid archers firing at an **orc** who has jammed both arms into the gate mechanism.

The orc can't move. Can't defend himself. Arrows thud into his flesh one after another. But he holds. His arms in the grinding gears are the only thing keeping the portcullis from crashing down, sealing the passage and trapping everyone outside.

Zacarías unleashes **Hellish Rebuke** — three rays of fire, one per archer. The blast doesn't kill them, but it shatters their magical shield. The archers spin around. Nixira's raven dives, pecking eyes, drawing fire. Robinson charges. The battle is brutal but brief.

They rush to the orc. He's shattered. Bones grinding in the mechanism. He shoves his arm deeper.

*"This is my last sacrifice. I don't think I have much time. The bastards cut the main rope — once my arms break, the gate falls. Go. There's no way to hold it."*

Nixira kneels beside him. *"You'll be remembered as a hero. What's your name, orc?"*

He pushes his head into the mechanism alongside his arms. His name is lost to the grinding of metal.

*"Your deeds will be written in the history books of this country. You'll be the greatest orc in the world."*

They go. Behind them, the gate holds — for now.

---

**Wyverns and War — The City Splits**

Wingless wyvern adults crash through behind the party. Iron gates slam down. Gelatina barriers erupt from the ground, sealing off entire blocks. In the chaos, the crowd surges — forty rebels pushing forward, dragging the PCs with them — and when the last gate falls, the party is **separated**.

Tidus is alone beyond the bridge, in the military Rivera district. Zacarías is trapped between walls and gelatina. Nixira is nearest the Mansion with Robinson.

Through the communication stone, Nixira reports to the rebel channel. Bixira — their distant ally — responds with one instruction: *"I don't care what you have to do. Get here. I can't face three Wyverns alone."*

Tidus looks toward the Mansion through elven eyes and sees her: **Nixira is mounted on a Wyvern**, daggers buried in its eyes, steering it by pain to crash into the other two. Fire erupts from all three beasts. She's winning — barely — but she cannot help them.

---

**The Rap Battle**

Before the separation, Nixira and Robinson face three draconid soldiers blocking the tower approach. Nixira is exhausted. Bleeding. Her face burns with the dwarven rune. She stretches, cracks her neck, and turns to Robinson.

*"You tell me when we start."*

Robinson grins. *"I've dreamed of this my entire life."*

They improvise. A **Vicious Mockery freestyle rap battle**, Nixira spitting insults while Robinson provides the beatbox rhythm.

*"Tú presumes que tienes sangre de dragón, pero realmente hueles a puro humo."*
*"He visto gusanos con más aliento que el tuyo. Ah, era fuego — pensé que estaba suspirando."*
*"Si sus ancestros volaran, agacharían la cabeza cada vez que los vieran."*

The psychic damage is lethal. One draconid clutches his throat, bleeding from the neck — Nixira's magic shredding his will to live. The second staggers, disoriented by the cascading insults. The third falls. Robinson scores a shield-strip that leaves them defenseless.

In the quiet after, Nixira remembers **Lili**. The songs they used to share. The memory hurts more than the rune on her face.

---

**The Rivera — Tidus Alone**

Tidus is trapped. The Rivera — the military river supply zone — stretches before him, full of enemy boats and soldiers. The sacred dragon lake nearby is enchanted with **Aguas Santas** (Holy Waters) that cause amnesia in non-draconic creatures. No passage there.

He's been in DragonLand for three days. Long enough that the draconic language is starting to click — words, phrases, the guttural commands. He can almost understand them now.

He spots a raft at the riverbank. Starts running. A dragon emerges from under a bridge. Dodges a gas bomb. Climbs a rooftop using a grappling rope latched to a gargoyle. Finds a heavy chest sitting on a trapdoor.

*"I don't have time for this. I'm not Zacarías."* He shoves the chest aside.

From beneath the trapdoor: *"¡Ayuda! ¡Ayuda!"* Someone is alive down there. But the trapdoor is rigged — a fuse mechanism that will detonate if cut wrong. Too high and the rope snaps, triggering the bomb. Too close to the igniter and Tidus lights it himself.

He draws his dagger. The session ends with the blade an inch from the mechanism.

---

**The Stone Invoker — Zacarías's Price**

Zacarías is cornered. Behind him: gelatina. Ahead: a **stone invoker** — a draconid elementalist who can manipulate earth and stone, pulling the ground itself toward enemies. These soldiers are nearly impossible to fight alone. Zacarías knows this.

He throws a stone as a distraction. The invoker turns — then feels the deception and pulls Nixira (who had been sneaking up from behind) toward him with the ground itself. She lands an 11-damage strike to his back.

Zacarías reveals himself. Tries to speak draconic — three days of immersion should count for something. The invoker doesn't understand. Zacarías switches to magic: *"Aléjate!"* The Command spell bounces off harmlessly. A mark glows on the draconid's forehead — **psychic immunity**. Mind control doesn't work on this one.

The fight goes badly. Zacarías attacks with Burning Hands — the draconid catches his **right hand** and rips it off.

*"¿Por qué era importante la mano? Porque soy un warlock."*

The hand that channels his pact magic. Gone. The power isn't lost — it just cost him the limb. He throws his remaining dagger with his left hand. Misses. Gets kicked in the face. Twice. His raven familiar claws at the invoker but takes damage. Zacarías is at **9 HP**, one-handed, bleeding, and being dragged across stone.

From somewhere nearby, Tidus's voice on the stone: *"I need help."*

---

### Key Revelations

- **Val, god of death**, has taken notice of Tidus. The assassination-by-mercy of Borax earned the first mark on a 20-count medallion. The purpose and consequences of reaching 20 are unknown.
- **Borax** was a DragonForce apprentice from Tidus's past — present in the castle on the morning Tidus killed Prince Roto. He made a demonic pact that traded his soul for power, and the talisman he wore was nearly full when Tidus destroyed it.
- **The dwarven curse** "asesina de niños" is a racial magical brand. Nixira now carries the permanent stigma of child-killer among all dwarves — a consequence of the fire ballista's collateral damage.
- **Zacarías lost his right hand** to the stone invoker. For a warlock who channels magic through gestures, this is potentially devastating.
- **Brasboredon** is structured into distinct zones: Robinson Bridge (entry), Cadalso (gallows/execution plaza), the Mansion (target), Commerce district, the Rivera (military river supply), and the Cuartel (barracks). The zones are separated by defense walls and gelatina barriers.
- **Los Limpiadores** (The Cleaners) are a neutral cross-faction cleanup crew, minimum level 10-12, who clear battlefields after combat. Nobody touches them.
- **Communication stones** (piedritas comunicadoras) allow the party to coordinate across the city — connected to Bixira's command channel.
- The party has been in DragonLand for approximately **3 days** — long enough for Zacarías to begin understanding spoken Draconic.

---

### Consequences

The party is separated across a city at war. Tidus is alone behind enemy lines with a bomb to defuse. Zacarías is one-handed and bleeding, fighting an elementalist he can't outmatch. Nixira carries a new curse on her face and the weight of dead children on her conscience. The orc who held the gate is dead or dying. And somewhere above the Mansion, their ally Bixira fights three wyverns alone, waiting for help that may never come.

The revolution isn't going well for either side. It's just going.
