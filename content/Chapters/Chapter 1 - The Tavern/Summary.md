# Chapter 1 - The Last Drop

**A night at the tavern turns deadly when old enemies come knocking and dark magic poisons the one dwarf holding the whole operation together.**

---

## Setting the Scene

The tavern is called *The Last Drop* — a desert outpost run by **Luney IcePeak**, a dwarven paladin-turned-operator with a gift for sheltering the difficult, the hunted, and the useful. Tonight, the place is packed: rowdy patrons, a demanding noble, drunks arguing over dwarven property rights, and a band warming up on the small stage. **Nixira Silversong**, a tiefling bard hiding under the alias *Mai Melody*, works the kitchen as the house cook. **Tidus**, an elven rogue, moves through the room as the waiter. **Zacarías**, a gnome warlock with blue eyes and a darker patron, watches the door. Nixira is supposed to perform later that night. None of them yet understand how expensive that performance will become.

---

## The Story

A scarred hunter steps into the tavern and the room goes still. He carries warrants, insignias, and the confidence of someone used to being obeyed. Luney receives him with cold courtesy and makes one thing clear: if he is going to ask questions, he will do it without tearing the tavern apart.

The hunter ignores the spirit of that request immediately. He begins interrogating table after table, pushing the room toward open panic. Zacarías quietly warns Tidus about the newcomer and gets shut down by Luney for being too obvious. In the kitchen, Nixira discovers that Luney has hidden **centipede essence** behind the bar — enough poison for two carefully selected targets.

Then Luney slides Zacarías a hidden note: *someone in the tavern needs to get out alive tonight.* The note changes everything. Tidus and Zacarías regroup, speak in Picón, and start reading the room the way Luney already has.

Luney's coded service diagrams mark which tables should receive "special orders." Nixira prepares the poison. Tidus will deliver it. One name is circled in red ink as the highest-priority target: **Kahel Varos**, a dangerous operative tied to **La Blanca**.

Everything detonates when Nixira takes the stage.

The instant her true name — **Nixira Silversong** — rings out across the room, one patron stiffens with fury. Rainbow recognizes her. So do others. Whatever protection anonymity once gave her is gone. Nixira refuses to flinch. She stares him down and begins to sing anyway.

Under cover of the music, Tidus delivers the poisoned drink to Kahel Varos as a "courtesy of the house." At the same time, Red's crew begins laying **gunpowder trails** through the tavern in a pattern Tidus recognizes immediately from his Dragonforce past: assassination by arson.

Luney reacts without hesitation. He reaches behind the bar, pulls a hidden lever, and **Protocol Three** activates: four concealed crossbows rise into firing position. But the room is crowded. Nixira pivots from performer to battlefield conductor, using her voice and stage presence to sweep patrons to the edges of the room and clear the firing lanes. Zacarías tries to ruin the gunpowder with water and instead crashes hard in the kitchen.

The bolts fire. One hits. Another kills **Buster**, an innocent patron sitting beside Kahel Varos. Now Kahel believes the attack was meant for him.

Then the room goes black.

One of Red's people smashes a vial of **Ghost Essence**. The darkness rolls across the tavern like living ink and seeks out the strongest spiritual presence in the room: **Luney IcePeak**. Without his former holy protections, he has no defense against it. He drops into a deathlike coma, and Protocol Three dies with his consciousness.

The rest is chaos. Zacarías uses illusion and sound to break the enemy's rhythm. Tidus works the dark like a knife. Nixira goes down, gets back up, and keeps fighting. The bald enforcer who brought the Ghost Essence is finally overwhelmed and killed. The tavern survives, but only barely.

---

## Key Revelations

- **Nixira Silversong is exposed.** Her real identity is now public, and that means the Veil of the Seven Songs can start closing in.
- **Tidus recognizes the Dragonforce arson pattern** because he has seen it from the inside before.
- **Zacarías reveals his connection to Malacor,** turning the night from a tavern incident into something much more personal.
- **Luney is far more dangerous than he first appears.** A tavern owner with hidden weapons, poison protocols, and enough authority to terrify monsters does not build that life by accident.

---

## Consequences

The party reaches Luney inside a dreamlike void — black, rippling, half-real. He gives them one instruction:

*"Before two quarter-moons pass, find a Quarter Emerald. Grind it. Feed it to me."*

He points them toward the river settlement or the elven mining camps. Then he fades.

Nixira also finds a list of **24 names** in Luney's pocket — witnesses, targets, debts, or something worse. Several dangerous people escape the night alive, including **Dracus** and the noble patron. The tavern closes with a sign on the door:

*"Please don't bother. Die somewhere else."*
