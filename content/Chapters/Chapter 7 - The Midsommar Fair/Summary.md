# Chapter 7: The Midsommar Fair — Session 07
## *"Even war-torn souls need a day to pretend they're ordinary."*

---

### Setting the Scene

Two days out of Brasboredon, the party rides in the cart of Robinson's son through dense forest, roughly fifteen to twenty kilometres from the Cordillera Oscura — the dark mountain range that separates DragonLand from the surrounding territories. They have been travelling for seven days total and have seven more ahead before reaching La Ultima Gota. The long rest on the road has healed some wounds, if not all scars. Zacarias still lacks a right hand. Nixira still carries the dwarven curse branded on her face. And Berlin — the Gremory goblin who accompanies them to ensure the Zafiro Esmeralda reaches Lunei — rides alongside, fastidious and easily offended.

Robinson's son slows the cart near a hidden village of freed slaves and refugees tucked into the forest. He has business at his father's nearby farms, but luck has delivered them something none of them expected: the Midsommar Fair, a travelling carnival set up by a community of outcasts. At the back of the fairgrounds, the tent of the Circulo de las Criaturas Inolvidables (the Circle of Unforgettable Creatures) — a legendary itinerant circus that brings exotic beasts from the distant islands — rises above the treeline. All three adventurers remember it from their pasts. Nixira once performed alongside its nymph musicians. Zacarias saw it as a child in his gnomish homeland. Even the soldiers of DragonForce used to scramble for tickets.

Robinson's son drops them off with a simple instruction: enjoy yourselves, and return by the end of the day.

It is eight in the morning. The DM has named this chapter *De Compras* — Shopping.

---

### The Story

**The Gate — Grimelda and the Golden Seal**

The party approached the fair's entrance: an enormous gate reinforced with magical wards and enchantments — a terrible idea to try to bypass. Behind a modest booth, a ticket-seller named Grimelda collected one gold coin per person. Upon touching the tickets, each one vanished and left a golden seal on the back of the bearer's hand — a simple golden ring that granted passage through the fair's magical barrier and offered protection within its boundaries.

Zacarias paid for himself and the others. Berlin tagged along grudgingly.

---

**The Arm-Wrestling Elf — Tidus and Vermillion**

Tidus charged ahead of the group, scanning for entertainment — or, more specifically, for attractive company. His perception roll landed on something unexpected: a beautiful elf woman seated at an arm-wrestling table. White skin, black robes, purple eyes, piercings along her pointed ears, silk garments marking her as a sorceress. She was physically trained but lean, not bulky. Across from her, a half-giant barbarian — nearly two meters sixty tall — strained with both hands against her single arm. She wasn't even gripping. Her palm lay flat and open while the barbarian pushed with everything he had.

*"Don't cheat,"* she said calmly. *"Next."*

Tidus sat down and made a wager: if he won, she would go out with him. She countered — if he managed it, she would give him a kiss right there.

He rolled a zero on strength. The elf hadn't even started.

Three rounds later, after increasingly desperate attempts, Tidus exhausted his strength modifier entirely. She flattened his hand against the table with a single movement and dismissed him with a smile. *"I appreciate the attempt, but... Next!"*

He paid his five copper and received something unexpected: a magical kiss. The spell allowed him to sense her location whenever he thought of her — a tracking enchantment disguised as a farewell. Her name was **Vermillion**. She was busy. She would reappear later.

---

**Odo's Jewels and the Gremory Incident**

Nixira and Zacarias found a jewelry stall run by **Odo**, an ancient elf with bark-like skin from a forest disease. His wares were mostly cheap iron trinkets painted gold — rings, chains, costume pieces — but among the baubles, Nixira spotted decorations for her horns: delicate chains that wrapped tight and wouldn't fall during combat, a paint that made flowers bloom along the horn's surface for twenty-four hours, and sharp crystal tips for the points.

Nixira bought the tips, a sun-emblem chain, and the flower paint. Fifteen copper. Pure cosmetics.

The trouble started when Berlin saw her shopping. The goblin's face contorted with betrayal — she had purchased from **the competition**. To a Gremory employee, this was an unforgivable insult. Berlin refused to speak to Nixira for the rest of the day.

Nixira tried persuasion. She whispered to Tidus that the Gremory family were overpriced thieves. Berlin overheard. He screamed *"Fake merchandise!"* and fled the stall at full sprint.

Zacarias chased him, commiserating: *"Can you believe it? That horrible Tiefling bought from the competition for her horrible horns."*

Berlin's offense deepened. The goblin vanished into the fairgrounds.

---

**The Weapons of Ahas — The Changeling Halfling**

The party arrived at a weapons stall run by a halfling so small — barely fifty centimetres — that they initially mistook him for a ball of hair. **Ahas** emerged from beneath his own beard, rechoncho and jovial, and announced his terms: platinum only.

His inventory was small but interesting. Laughing axes enchanted by a bard at a comedy festival — every strike caused the victim to laugh, generating attacks of opportunity. Two greatswords with left-handed and right-handed grips, their edges stained with the green blood of Ilfang, Lord of the Kobolds. Standard longswords, one inscribed with the phrase *"Nada escapa al circulo"* (Nothing escapes the circle). And his greatest treasure: a silver dagger displayed under a crystal dome.

The **Daga Plateada** (Silver Dagger) — also called "La Sin Huesos" (The Boneless) — dissolved the first bone it struck. Ahas demonstrated by stabbing a field mouse's leg, which went limp as gelatin. Thirty-two gold pieces. It came with a magic-resistant ivory sheath that protected it from destruction spells.

Zacarias wanted it. Tidus wanted it. Ahas suggested they settle ownership with an archery contest at booth nine. Zacarias agreed, but they couldn't afford the trip across the fair — and Ahas only accepted platinum.

When Tidus mentioned that Nixira had killed children, Ahas's demeanor changed instantly. The halfling expanded — three metres tall, rippling with muscle. He was no halfling. He was a **changeling**, and he grabbed Zacarias by his stump and lifted him off the ground: *"In this shop, all races are respected. Get out."*

He shrunk back to his diminutive form and resumed selling to other customers.

---

**The Dragon Wand Shop — Payville Heberdort**

The next stall was run by **Payville Heberdort**, an ancient dwarf woman — a retired adventurer with scars from killing black dragons. Her husband Brunil had been close friends with the IcePeak family. She maintained a collection of used wands, each partially depleted:

- **Wand of Aerosol** (9/50 charges): Made anything it targeted infinitely heavy for one minute. 135 gold.
- **Wand of Healing for Biting Wounds** (32/50 charges): Healed burn injuries but tended to bite the user. 2,880 gold — crafted from the mouth of a Crimson Horror Cyst, a level-40 false dragon. Its healing was total: it restored the user to their ideal physical form, rebuilding limbs, reversing aging to peak condition.
- **Wand of Levitation** (34/50 charges): Thirty seconds of simple levitation per use.
- **Wand of Mirror Image** (18/50 charges): Created a visual clone that lasted until another wand touched it. 1,620 gold.
- **Wand of Monster Summoning** (19/50 charges): Summoned abyssal creatures with — according to Payville — *"an insatiable sexual desire."* Reserved for female clients only.

Tidus asked about the healing wand's capacity to restore limbs. Payville confirmed: the Crimson Horror Cyst's magic reconfigured the user's entire physical essence to their mental ideal. Zacarias — missing a hand — listened very carefully.

The prices were far beyond their means. Payville mentioned her husband Brunil had been intimate friends with Lunei. A memory surfaced unbidden in Tidus's mind: walking in on Lunei and Payville engaged in a magical long-distance encounter of an extremely graphic nature. The image was seared permanently into his consciousness.

---

**Winu's Variety Shop — The Pirate Merchant**

At the next stall, **Winu** — a sun-damaged elf with a Dali moustache, pirate insignia on his chest, and coloured spheres dangling from his belt — sold a mixed inventory: potions, holy water vials, jade stones, miniature enchanted furniture, drums from an uncontacted island civilization, and bottles.

Nixira negotiated a 30% discount on healing potions after bonding with Winu over their shared love of the **Velo de las Siete Canciones** (Veil of the Seven Songs). Winu had served under a vampire captain whose favourite music was the Veil's songs — particularly those of **Draxa**. He had practiced them endlessly, especially a duet called *El Soneto del Mar Eterno* that featured **Nixira Silverson**.

Nixira recognized the name. It was her own. She hummed the tune. Winu's eyes lit up — *"You know the work? One of the lesser stars of the group, but passable."*

The word *lesser* stung. But Nixira kept her composure.

Winu also offered passage: his captain had gone to **X'Mintang**, a vampiric conclave settlement, and had not returned in five overdue days. He needed crew. If the party met him in one decana (ten days) at the port of **DragonNext**, he would take them aboard. He handed Nixira a sphere that produced the sound of ocean waves — a communication device linked to the DragonNext docks.

Three **Superior Healing Potions** (8d4+8 each) for 90 gold total, after the discount. One for each party member.

Berlin, however, refused to contribute — still furious about the Gremory insult. He ran off again.

---

**The Oracle — Three Riddles, Three Truths**

They entered a dark tent where an ancient sage sat in meditation, eyes closed, surrounded by cushions. He spoke without opening his eyes: he was connected to the Arcanos Superiores — the Higher Arcana — and could answer one question per person about past, present, or future. The price: solve his riddle.

**Riddle for Zacarias**: *"I have rivers without water. Towns built without a single brick. Forests without wood. Islands without sand. Entire continents without people. What am I?"*

Zacarias meditated. His patron Malfas whispered through the void. The letter M appeared in fire. He could not solve it alone. The answer — **a map** (*mapa*) — came when Nixira spoke it aloud. The sage accepted.

**The sage's answer to Zacarias**: *"The iguana, as you call it, is only the first part of this conspiracy. The archangel and his leader, the one who guised himself as Nixira — they have much to do with it. If you ever reach the astral plane, I would personally drive a dagger into the heart of the ravens."* When pressed about other traitors: *"All of them — the true traitor — all of them were simply subjugated by something older than time itself. Seek the Lord of Bodies and you will find the answer. Not even the Arcana know such secrets."*

**Riddle for Nixira and Tidus**: *"I have keys but open no doors. I have space but no rooms. You can enter but you cannot leave. What am I?"*

The sage watched Nixira specifically. She answered: *"A musical score."* (*Partitura*.) Keys (claves), spaces (silencios), entry (compases). Correct.

**The sage's answer to Nixira**: *"You face a sad future. One of your companions must give you information — too valuable, too precise. You think you renounced the Veil of the Seven Songs, but you were manipulated into leaving. Everything was arranged so you would distance yourself because you were too dangerous. You have nothing to redeem, Nixira. It is not yet time for you to know the truth. Only know that when the moment comes — when you face the throne, when you hear the seventh song — end the spell. Do not perpetuate that evil."*

**Riddle for Tidus**: *"I am always hungry. I must always eat. But if you give me water, I will die. What am I?"*

*"Fire,"* Tidus answered immediately.

**The sage's answer to Tidus**: *"Tell me who the traitors are."* The sage's response revealed that the traitors in Tidus's past were connected to a scientist named **Dral** who worked for Dragon Force and had attempted to create chimeras — ultimately trying to fuse his own five-year-old daughter into one. Tidus had been sent by the **Delta Force** to investigate and had killed him.

Each party member received an **inspiration point** for engaging with the riddles.

---

**Horse Races and Gambling**

The fair offered magical horse racing — four enchanted steeds (Diamond, Clubs, Hearts, Spades) running a kilometre-long illusory track visible through a magical window. Bets paid ten-to-one.

First race: Tidus bet on Hearts (1 gold). Zacarias bet on Spades (8 gold). Nixira bet on Hearts (1 gold). The cards turned. Tidus's horse won. He collected 10 gold. Zacarias lost 8.

Second race: Zacarias bet on Hearts again. The Ace of Diamonds appeared — his horse stumbled before the gate even opened. Rigged. The party declared the races crooked and left. Tidus pickpocketed a gold coin from Zacarias with Sleight of Hand (rolled with advantage: 8+5=13 vs Constitution) and handed it to Nixira. Zacarias never noticed.

---

**The Potion-Brewing Workshop**

A travelling potion master offered a free workshop: prepare one potion from raw ingredients. Three recipes available — Superior Healing Potion, Invincible Strength Potion (4d20 unstoppable melee damage in one hit), and Perpetual Invisibility Potion (24 hours of at-will invisibility).

Each participant had five attempts to toss their ingredients into a cauldron.

**Tidus** failed all five throws. His cauldron filled with pestilent slime — he had confused rosemary with basil and accidentally brewed a Stench Potion. The master dismissed him: *"Not your calling, boy. Come back in two hours."*

**Zacarias** attempted the Invisibility Potion. He got close — reached the precise step where the potion should have dematerialized — but confused the essence of Asmodeus with the essence of Phospheus. The result: a **Counter-Invisibility Potion** that made anything it touched flicker in and out of visibility at random. His remaining hand turned invisible and began strobing. He tried to pocket the potion; the professor slapped him, called him a thief, and banned him from the workshop.

**Nixira** attempted the Invincible Strength Potion. She got further than the others — her brew smelled exactly right, like the finest dishes she used to cook at La Ultima Gota. But she produced a **Euphoria Potion** — an illegal substance that created false courage. The professor confiscated and destroyed it, complimenting her aptitude: *"You have talent for potions. Come back in two hours."*

---

**The Hammer of Strength**

A strength-test game: slam an iron dwarven hammer down on a plate and ring the bell. Ten ranks from *"Hijo de Flor"* (Son of a Flower) to *"Dios del Martillo"* (God of the Hammer). Hero rank or above paid 500 gold.

Zacarias rolled a 1. He couldn't even lift the hammer. The crowd chanted: *"Hijo de Flor! Hijo de Flor!"*

Tidus rolled an 8. *"Delicada Princesa."* (Delicate Princess.) Respectable for someone his size, but nowhere near the bell.

Nixira shoved both of them aside, grabbed the hammer, and slammed it. Her roll was strong enough to reach the upper tiers. The crowd roared.

---

**The Tavern — Crazy Hour and Prophetic Drinks**

By noon, the party reached the fair's bar just as "Crazy Hour" opened — a twice-daily event with three special cocktails:

- **Lord Vintage**: A black sparkling wine that tasted like childhood memories and provoked warm, conspiratorial laughter.
- **Ponche Azul** (Blue Punch): A black, viscous whiskey-vodka blend tasting of caramel that induced oniric visions — controllable prophetic dreams.
- **Ether de Porrit**: A green beer with the aroma of Nixira's finest cooking, causing extreme vertigo but granting **triple advantage** (roll three d20s, keep highest) on the next action.

Nixira proposed they roll dice to determine their drinks randomly. Each took a d4.

Zacarias received the **Ether de Porrit** — the triple-advantage drink. He stumbled immediately, dizzy but blessed with supernatural luck.

Tidus received the **Ponche Azul**. He drank it in one shot and collapsed face-first onto the table. Three visions hit him:

1. **Lunei** — someone placing the Zafiro Esmeralda crystal in his mouth, squeezing his jaw to make him swallow it.
2. The party on a ship, watching the banner of DragonLand recede into the distance.
3. The party disembarking at a new location, with the absolute certainty that **disguises would be required**.

Nixira received a plain black beer. One sip turned her cheeks red. She was a terrible drinker.

They ordered more rounds. Each magical drink added +2 Charisma (stacking to +6 after three), but imposed a d6 penalty on all rolls for three hours. Tidus wisely stopped them before the fourth drink — which would have knocked them unconscious.

The party was drunk. Gloriously, catastrophically drunk.

---

**The Talent Show — Drunk and Magnificent**

The travelling circus held a talent competition. Each party member entered separately.

**Zacarias** performed a magic show. Despite being one-handed, drunk, and heckled by children chanting *"Manco! Manco!"* (One-hand!), he summoned his raven familiar for aerial tricks and cast illusions through his remaining fingers. The crowd initially jeered, then fell silent as the magic took hold. His score was solid — 24 points across three rounds of judging.

**Tidus** performed an acrobatic dagger show. Vermillion — the arm-wrestling elf sorceress — appeared from the audience, drunk and eager, volunteering as his assistant. She stood against a board while Tidus threw daggers around her silhouette. He rolled brilliantly: 16, 18, 13 across three rounds — perfect scores from all judges. 30 points. The crowd erupted. Vermillion's revealing attire did not hurt the presentation.

**Nixira** registered a fire-and-blood performance. She filled out the safety form with clinical detail: *"There will be fireworks. There is a risk of fire. It will appear to rain blood, but it is an illusion."* She requested Raining Blood as her entrance music. Her show was the finale.

---

**Tidus and Zacarias — A Conversation Between Rogues**

While Nixira prepared her performance, Tidus and Zacarias sat together in the fairgrounds and shared something rare between them: honesty.

Tidus told Zacarias about his past with the Delta Force — a covert operations unit. He described his darkest mission: investigating a DragonForce scientist named Dral who was creating chimeras. Dral had attempted to fuse his own five-year-old daughter into a chimeric creature. Tidus killed him.

*"Sometimes there are people who deserve to die,"* Zacarias said quietly.

Zacarias reciprocated. He spoke of growing up gnomish — always feeling small, always feeling lesser against races that lived centuries and towered above him. But he had decided that even as a gnome, he could accomplish great things. That conviction led him to the followers of Malfas — the **Hermandad de la Garra Sangrante** (Brotherhood of the Bleeding Claw) — where race didn't matter. Where an elf friend with features similar to Tidus had first welcomed him.

Tidus listened. For a moment, the rogue and the warlock were not combatants or competitors. They were two broken men at a carnival, pretending the world outside didn't exist.

*"Elves and dragons are the same,"* Tidus said. *"Proud. They think they're superior. But they're no different. A plague."*

*"And gnomes?"* Zacarias asked.

*"Ugly. But I like them."*

The sincerity was imperfect, but it was real.

---

**Nixira's Vision — Draxa and the Veil**

After drinking her Ponche Azul, Nixira received her own prophetic visions:

1. **Draxa** was being punished — flogged — by Calium (Keylan) for failing to capture Nixira at Brasboredon.
2. Draxa stood alongside four other members of the **Velo de las Siete Canciones**, preparing for something. Behind them: the banner of **DragonNext**.
3. **Berlin** — the Gremory goblin — kneeling before an unseen figure, saying: *"My lord, I wish to see you, but I regret to inform you that your grandfather has died."*

The visions confirmed: the Veil was not idle. Draxa was being mobilized. And Berlin carried secrets the party had not yet uncovered.

### Key Revelations

- **Nixira was manipulated into leaving the Veil of the Seven Songs** — the sage's prophecy indicates she did not leave voluntarily. She was expelled because she was considered too dangerous. The truth has not yet been revealed to her.
- **The Seventh Song is a spell** — when Nixira faces the throne and hears it, she must end it. The sage's warning implies the Veil's power is sustained by a magical song that perpetuates some form of evil.
- **The Lord of Bodies** is the true orchestrator behind the conspiracies affecting the party. Even the Higher Arcana cannot fully comprehend this entity. Zacarias has been told to seek it.
- **Draxa is being punished by Calium** for her failure at Brasboredon. She and four other Veil members are assembling at DragonNext — the same port where Winu's pirate captain is expected.
- **Berlin's grandfather has died** — a vision suggesting a power shift within the Gremory family that may affect the party's standing.
- **Tidus served the Delta Force** — a covert operations unit. His darkest mission involved killing a scientist who was creating chimeras from his own daughter.
- **Zacarias joined the Brotherhood of the Bleeding Claw** (Hermandad de la Garra Sangrante) — followers of Malfas where all races were accepted.
- **The Wand of Healing** at Payville's shop can restore Zacarias's hand — it rebuilds the user to their ideal physical form. At 2,880 gold, it is far beyond their current means.
- **Winu offers passage to X'Mintang** in one decana from the port of DragonNext — a potential future quest hook involving a missing vampire captain.
- **Vermillion** is a powerful elf sorceress who gave Tidus a tracking enchantment disguised as a kiss.

---

### Consequences

- **The Veil is hunting them at DragonNext** — Draxa and four agents are assembling at the exact port Winu invited them to visit. Accepting the pirate's offer means walking into the Veil's staging ground.
- **Berlin is not just a merchant escort** — his vision of kneeling before a lord and reporting a death suggests deeper Gremory family politics at play. His silence toward Nixira may not last.
- **Zacarias's hand can be restored** — but only if they can afford 2,880 gold or find Payville Heberdort again. The dwarf woman travels with the fair.
- **The party is drunk** — +6 Charisma but -d6 on all rolls for three hours, followed by four hours of hangover. Any combat encounter during this window would be extremely dangerous.
- **The talent show results** may yield prize money — Tidus scored 30 points with perfect marks, potentially winning the competition.
- **Nixira carries a new communication device** — Winu's sphere connects to the DragonNext docks. A lifeline, or a trap.
- **Tidus's prophetic visions** confirm the Zafiro Esmeralda must be physically consumed by Lunei (placed in his mouth and crushed), that they will leave DragonLand by ship, and that disguises will be needed at their destination.

- **Who:** Goblin employee of House Gremory. Accompanies the party to verify delivery of the Zafiro Esmeralda to Lunei.
- **What he did:** Paid for entry to the fair. Became deeply offended when Nixira purchased cosmetics from a competitor stall. Fled the party twice, refusing all communication. Was seen in Nixira's prophetic vision kneeling before a figure, reporting his grandfather's death.
- **What happened to him:** Disappeared into the fair. Briefly appeared at the talent show when Vermillion pushed past him. His emotional state remains volatile.
- **Related to:** House Gremory, Lunei (mission objective), Nixira (conflict), the unnamed Gremory lord (vision).
- **End status:** **Alive** — at the fair, not speaking to the party.
