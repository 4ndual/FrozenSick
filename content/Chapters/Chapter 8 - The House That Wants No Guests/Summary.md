# Chapter 8 — The House That Wants No Guests

**"The dead do not haunt — they remember. And the living, in their grief, give those memories teeth."**

---

## Setting the Scene

The guild of the Calvo Manco — the Bald One-Hand — is barely days old, and already its founders are hunting for their first mission. Back at La Ultima Gota, the magical notice board beneath the tavern-fortress has populated with four postings. Zacarias, restless and eager, pulls the red-flagged notice from the board one evening and reads it aloud to his companions: *"The House That Wants No Guests. Property inspection requested in High Rock. Seeking persons willing to spend one night in my house, investigate the strange events, and determine if there is real danger. Avoid priests and authorities. Ask for Circaven by the old aqueduct. Reward: 120 gold pieces and preferential purchase option."*

High Rock — Piedra Alta — is a speck of a village so insignificant it does not appear on most maps, perched on a cliff in the mountainous Rumbergill Peninsula south of the tavern. Three days by borrowed cart, the party rides into grey skies, thinning forests, and a settlement that feels more like a tomb than a town. The villagers seal their doors at the sight of outsiders. No shops are open. No guards are visible. Even the tavern keeper, a blind, ancient draconic named Borco, serves his bile-colored cerveza through cataracts and resignation.

This is the party's first guild mission — proof of concept for the Calvo Manco. The reward is modest, but the real prize is reputation, and perhaps the option to purchase the mansion itself. What awaits them inside the Ben family estate is something no adventurer has survived a full night to explain.

---

## The Story

**The Ghost Town — Arrival at High Rock**

The cart crested the final ridge and the world changed. Green gave way to grey. The sun, even at its peak, barely penetrated the mountain walls surrounding the village. A cold, oppressive atmosphere pressed down on the party as they entered the commercial district at roughly three in the afternoon.

Doors slammed shut. Shutters clattered into place. The entire village sealed itself at the sight of three strangers arriving by cart. Not a single establishment remained open. Windows became eyes — watching, wary, unwelcoming.

*"Nobody wants to talk to us,"* Nixira observed, scanning the empty streets. *"And there's no guard force in sight."*

Tidus studied the map Lunei had provided. High Rock was built on a cliff with only three access roads, each marked by a watchtower. The points of interest were few: the old mill, the commercial zone, and high on the ridge above them all, the Ben family mansion.

**The Tavern — Borco and the First Warnings**

They found the only open establishment through sheer perception — a faint light leaking from a door two blocks north. Inside, the tavern was a Viking-style hall stripped of all comfort: bare wooden tables, simple chairs, and at the far end, illuminated by a single thermal flame, an ancient draconic man polishing the inside of a jar.

Borco was old. If he were human, he would have been seventy or eighty. His eyes were aquamarine — and blind with cataracts. He did not recognize them, did not care to, and served them fermented bile that passed for beer and bread so stale it had become simultaneously soggy and hard.

Tidus tried charm. Zacarias paid from the party funds — then Tidus used Sleight of Hand to steal the coins back from Zacarias's pocket to cover the cost. The old draconic did not notice the theft, but he noticed the gold.

*"In five years, I haven't seen coins like these,"* Borco said, suddenly attentive. *"If you're smart, don't show that kind of wealth in these lands."*

When pressed about the mansion, Borco's demeanor darkened. He lifted his collar to reveal a green scar — crystalline, almost frozen — that pulsed faintly before returning to his natural scales.

*"It's real,"* he said quietly. *"In all my long years, if there's one thing I've learned from the world of the dead, it's that they are here."*

He directed them to find Circaven at a seamstress shop three blocks behind his tavern, run by a woman named Lina. He warned them about the approaching storm, about the curfew, and about a contingent of guards that had been dispatched to Brasboredon three days prior — leaving the village virtually unprotected.

**Lina the Seamstress — Tea, Traps, and Treachery**

Lina's shop was a modest house with eight locks on the door and four padlocks in the corners. The old draconic seamstress was suspicious, hostile, and — as the party quickly discovered — a petty thief running a sleeping-poison operation with Dask the miller and Borco himself. She served them tea laced with a soporific plant. Tidus noticed the poison through perception when his skin began to sweat green, and he discretely poured his tea into a nearby potted plant.

Zacarias failed his constitution save and collapsed onto the sofa, incoherent and drooling.

*"His medicine!"* Tidus improvised, telling Lina that Zacarias had been cursed by a warlock named Malacor and needed a specific compound. The deception check tied at eighteen — Lina was too experienced to be fooled easily but too flustered to call the bluff outright. While she ran upstairs to find supplies, Tidus searched her belongings and discovered something valuable: a Barca wool cape, recognizable by the purple vapor it emitted near heat. The legendary fabric from Love Dread sheep could withstand temperatures thirty degrees below zero.

When Lady Circaven finally arrived, the situation resolved itself. The noblewoman swept in with aristocratic authority, immediately identified Lina's poisoned tea, covered the toxic plant with a cloth, and dismissed the seamstress's schemes as a known nuisance. *"Lina is a petty pickpocket. She and Dask drug travelers, steal their goods, and have Borco claim they got drunk. Modus operandi for years. Not dangerous — just pathetic."*

**Lady Circaven Ben — The Client**

Circaven was the last of the Ben family — ancient draconic nobility, roughly 1,700 years old in draconic terms, the equivalent of a human in her late seventies. Without her formal attire she was simply an exhausted old woman with thinning scales, grey roots beneath dyed hair, and a threadbare nightgown that betrayed the poverty behind her public facade.

She spoke in Old Draconic — formal, archaic, difficult even for native speakers to parse. She was the leader of the village council, the last holdout against the city of Clastis absorbing High Rock into its jurisdiction. Her ancestral jewels were nearly depleted, spent on maintaining the village's independence and paying adventurer after adventurer to investigate her mansion.

*"I don't know if you believe in the beyond,"* she told them in the study. *"Many call me crazy, many say I've lost my mind. But I am one hundred percent certain that the spirit of my daughter still resides in this house. It has almost taken it over."*

Her story unfolded: approximately ten years ago, during a council meeting with Borco, Olek, and Lina, they had been playing with tarot cards brought by a traveling adventurer. While the adults played, two children — Circaven's daughter Valca and Olek's son — disappeared from an upstairs room. The window was open. No tracks were found. No one in the watchtowers saw them leave.

Since that night, the mansion had been alive. Voices. Crying. Furniture moving on its own. Windows opening and closing like eyelids. A cold that defied the fire in the hearth. Circaven had hired every kind of adventurer — paladins, monks, necromancers, tiefling conjurers, exorcists of every faith. One group, the Cofradía de los 14, lasted three hours before fleeing. A tiefling had tried to summon a demon — it lasted six hours inside the mansion before it too fled in terror.

She gave them keys to every room, warned them she slept with a dagger under her pillow, and retired upstairs.

**Dask the Miller — Confrontation on the Road**

Before reaching the mansion, the party needed bread — Circaven's errand. They found Dask at the mill, loading his donkey and hiding daggers inside his cart. Zacarias confronted him too aggressively, calling him a thief before any diplomacy had been attempted. The miller nearly drew his weapons.

Nixira intervened with Thaumaturgy, projecting her voice with authority. *"We come from Lady Ben. We know your arrangements. We don't care — just don't interfere with our work."*

After a tense negotiation, Dask sold them a dozen loaves at the inflated "foreigner price" of two silver pieces. Before leaving, he offered his own warning about the mansion: he had personally entered it once, years after the fateful night, to humor the old woman. Something dark moved beneath the floors. He felt it breathe. He left before the second bell and never returned.

*"The house smiled at me,"* he whispered, gripping his donkey's reins with trembling hands.

**Approach to the Mansion — Ravens and Preparations**

The storm was coming. Zacarias cast Speak with Animals and rallied a flock of local ravens, appointing his summoned raven as their commander. The wild ravens' alpha — marked by a single white feather — attacked Zacarias's familiar, gouging out one eye before departing with its flock. The party learned that all animals were fleeing the area. Whatever was in the mansion, even the wildlife knew to avoid it.

Zacarias cast a medicine check on Dask's crystalline symptoms. The word came to him immediately: *Frozen*. The same plague that had haunted their entire campaign.

The party took stock of their resources and developed a protocol: Tidus would handle physical investigation — traps, alarms, securing rooms. Nixira and Zacarias would handle the supernatural. If it was an illusion, Tidus could defend against it through self-deception. If it came from the infernal planes, Zacarias would manage it. If it required magical cleansing, Nixira would play her role.

They arrived at the mansion at five o'clock, just as the storm broke.

**The Mansion — First Night**

The Ben estate sat atop a barren hill surrounded by dead forest, shrouded in thickening fog. Through the ornate vitral windows — crafted by the same artisan who had worked on a royal palace — Tidus could make out two suits of armor flanking a main corridor, a long carpet, and a bifurcating staircase.

Circaven greeted them in her nightgown. *"Welcome. I was expecting you."*

The exploration began in structured turns. Tidus set up alarm systems with rope and bells at every door, scattered ball bearings across the dining room floor to detect physical intrusions, and spread flour on the vestibule to reveal invisible presences. Nixira and Zacarias performed rituals in the study — a blood circle drawn on the floor before the fireplace, channeling energy through clasped hands while Nixira played her harp.

The house responded.

Eleven chairs scraped across the dining room floor simultaneously. Cold air pulsed from the fireplace despite a roaring fire. When Nixira drew her blood circle, the mansion absorbed it — the blood evaporated from the floor, draining her vitality. Footsteps sounded behind them in empty corridors. The portrait of a young draconic girl on the staircase wall shifted its gaze from left to facing forward when no one was watching.

**The Diary and the Secret Basement**

Tidus discovered a diary — *Diary of Ben Dormit the Third* — partially burned, written in Old Draconic. It dated to approximately a thousand years prior. The ancestor who built the mansion had done so over a cemetery of defeated commercial rivals. He had exorcised the grounds, sealed the basement, and spent his life trying to prevent his wife from accessing it. The wife had become obsessed with necromancy — specifically, with speaking to the dead.

*"My father insists a basement is a good idea,"* the diary read. *"As soon as I can, I will seal all its entrances."*

The party now knew: there was a hidden basement. Circaven had no knowledge of it.

**Visions, Death, and Discovery**

The supernatural events intensified. Zacarias touched the door of the child's room and received a flood of visions — fourteen monks performing an exorcism, a boy and girl leaping from a window, three orcs with massive clubs battering the walls, encloaked thieves looting the lady's room. All from different eras, all layered simultaneously, centuries of attempts to deal with the mansion compressed into a single hallucinatory moment.

In one of the upstairs bedrooms, Zacarias lay on a bed to test it. The mattress swallowed him — he sank through and was teleported to the child's room across the hall. Beneath the beds, they found ritual circles painted on the floor: amateur teleportation spells, badly constructed.

Nixira found the key. In the child's nursery, a crib sat on a conspicuously new circular carpet. Inside the crib, among the mobile's ornaments, was a green gemstone shaped like a leaf. And in the portrait on the staircase wall, the young draconic girl wore a necklace with a green pendant — with an empty socket where a gem should sit.

Meanwhile, Zacarias attempted a ritual invoking his connection to the infernal circles to detect demonic presence. His patron Malfas responded directly — a raven's eye appeared at the cracked window during a lightning flash, and Malfas's voice thundered through his mind: *"What are you doing here? Why do you work for a stupid mortal who tries to imitate a fraction of our power? Disgraceful."* The psychic backlash killed Zacarias. His ninth death.

His death saves succeeded. He returned, as he always did — ascending from the lowest circles of the underworld, catching a glimpse of the ritual markings that existed beneath the mansion's foundation on his way back to the living.

**The Uroboros Ring**

Tidus, using Sleight of Hand during his reconnaissance of Circaven's bedroom, stole the ring from her sleeping finger. It bore the Uroboros — a dragon biting its own tail, the sigil of the Ben family, believers in eternity. The ring was the key: when placed in the portrait's empty socket, the painting descended into the floor, revealing a hidden staircase leading down to the sealed basement.

But first, the ring revealed its true power. When Tidus wore it in the basement's ritual circle, he saw the face of young Lunei — not because he had ever known Lunei as a child, but because the ring accessed the wearer's deepest memories and projected them. When Nixira put it on, the sacrificial altars ignited with flame, and thousands upon thousands of faces materialized — screaming, desperate, pleading for help. Not ghosts. Memories. Residual impressions burned into the house by centuries of failed rituals and the Ben family's original necromantic obsession.

**The Truth in the Basement**

The basement was ancient — sealed for at least a thousand years. The ritual circles on the floor were crudely painted with a mixture of paint and animal blood rather than human blood. The sacrificial altars held the bones of a lamb and a rabbit — animals that had no place in proper ritual sacrifice. Two altars, twelve points in the summoning circle, and the overwhelming conclusion from Zacarias's arcane knowledge: someone who understood nothing about magic had attempted to speak with the dead. They had copied rituals from books without comprehension, mixed necromancy with communion rites, and created something that no trained practitioner could untangle — because the errors themselves had become the system.

Each subsequent adventurer, with their own rituals and invocations, had layered more contradictory magic on top. The mansion had become a palimpsest of failed spells, each one amplifying the disturbance.

**The Resolution**

The party confronted Circaven. They woke her — and when she rose, the supernatural phenomena ceased immediately. The storm became audible again. The unnatural darkness retreated. Stars appeared in the windows.

*"I regret to inform you,"* Tidus told her, *"that the curse of the house is you."*

It was not the mansion that was haunted. It was Lady Ben herself. The Uroboros ring, combined with the ancient rituals beneath the house and the accumulation of every adventurer's botched magic, had turned her into a living conduit. When she slept, her subconscious mind — shaped by grief, obsession, and the ring's memory-projection power — animated the house with residual impressions of everyone who had ever entered it.

They brought her to the basement. She had no idea it existed. Reading her ancestor's diary, she learned the truth: the original Lady Ben — her ancestress, generations removed — had been obsessed with necromancy. Her husband had built the basement to contain her experiments, then sealed it. But the obsession had been passed down through generations. Eleven daughters of the Ben line had died of Frozen Sickness before reaching adulthood. Eleven spirits, bound to the mansion by their mother's desperate rituals to keep their memory alive. Not malevolent — just trapped.

The diary's final entry spoke of a son, Bran, sent to Najden to negotiate peace and break the family curse. After that, silence.

Circaven wept.

The party spent the rest of the night in systematic cleanup — erasing ritual circles, removing magical traps left by previous adventurers, dismantling the haphazard enchantments that had accumulated over centuries. They could not break the original binding that held the eleven spirits, but they neutralized everything else. The mansion would still contain the Ben family ghosts, but they were not dangerous — only sad.

Dawn arrived. The storm had passed. Sunlight reached High Rock for what felt like the first time. The villagers were outside, children playing in the streets, doors open. Borco stood in his tavern entrance and watched the three adventurers walk down from the mansion alive. He breathed deeply.

*"You survived."*

*"Tell everyone,"* Tidus said. *"The Calvo Manco was here, and your prayers have been answered."*

Two young bards scrambled onto a table to compose a song. Nixira winced at how bad it was — she would need to write them a proper ballad later.

They collected their 120 gold pieces. Circaven offered the mansion at a heavily discounted price of 20,000 gold — with a purchase agreement that reserved it exclusively for the guild. She would remain as caretaker until they could afford it.

*"You have my word,"* she said. *"The Ben family keeps its promises."*

Three days back to La Ultima Gota. Seven days total. Their first mission, completed.

## Key Revelations

- **The Ben family curse is tied to Frozen Sickness** — Eleven daughters of the Ben line died of the same crystalline plague that has haunted the party since Session 1. The disease appears to be far older and more widespread than previously understood.
- **Circaven is not a villain but a victim** — Her ancestor's necromantic obsession, the Uroboros ring, and centuries of accumulated magical interference combined to make her an unwitting conduit for supernatural phenomena. She had no knowledge of the basement or its rituals.
- **The mansion sits atop a sealed necromantic laboratory** — Built over a cemetery, with a basement designed to contain a noblewoman's amateur attempts at speaking with the dead. The rituals were too poorly constructed to work as intended but too potent to be harmless.
- **Adventurers made everything worse** — Every guild, order, and freelancer who attempted to "fix" the mansion layered more contradictory magic onto an already unstable system. The interference became the curse itself.
- **Zacarias's patron Malfas is displeased** — The demon lord directly intervened to punish Zacarias for working in a mansion contaminated with amateur necromancy, calling it an insult to true infernal power. This marks Malfas's most direct and hostile communication yet.
- **The Calvo Manco guild has its first potential headquarters** — The Ben mansion is available for 20,000 gold with a purchase agreement in place. It comes with eleven friendly ghosts.

---

## Consequences

- **The guild's reputation is born** — High Rock now knows the Calvo Manco. Borco will spread the word. Two bards are composing a (terrible) ballad. The guild earned 3 reputation points out of the 10 needed for advancement.
- **The mansion purchase agreement is active** — Circaven has reserved the property exclusively for the guild at 20,000 gold. No other buyer can claim it, but the party cannot enter until payment is complete.
- **Frozen Sickness connections deepen** — The Ben family's generational plague mirrors the disease affecting Lunei and others. The party has not yet made this connection explicitly, but the evidence is accumulating.
- **Malfas's patience is fraying** — Zacarias died his ninth death during this mission, and his patron personally condemned his choices. The warlock's relationship with his infernal benefactor may be approaching a breaking point.
- **Lina, Dask, and Borco remain in High Rock** — The petty criminal ring is exposed but unpunished. Circaven tolerates them. They could become minor allies or nuisances in future visits.
- **The eleven Ben spirits remain bound** — The party cleaned the accumulated magical debris but could not free the eleven daughters. This remains an open thread for a future return.
