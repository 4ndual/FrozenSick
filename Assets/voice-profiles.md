# Voice Profiles — Speaker Attribution Reference

Detailed speech-pattern descriptions for identifying who is speaking in raw session transcripts.

---

## The DM (Frank/Franklin)

**Role:** Narrates the world, voices ALL NPCs, adjudicates rules, describes consequences.

### Linguistic Signals

**Second-person narration (strongest signal):**
- "Ves como..." / "Ves que..."
- "Te das cuenta de que..."
- "Alcanzas a ver..."
- "Sientes..."
- "Escuchas..."
- "Empiezas a notar..."

**Turn prompts:**
- "[Character name] qué quieres hacer?"
- "Qué quieres hacer?"
- "Listo, tu turno."
- "Siguiente."

**Dice requests:**
- "Lanza un dado de 20"
- "Lanza tus dos dados de 10"
- "La prueba es [number]"
- "Dado de 20 contra mí"

**World events:**
- "Eventos del mundo mundial"
- "Justo en ese momento..."
- Scene transitions: "Justo enfrente de ustedes..."

**Rule explanations:**
- "Eso cuenta como una acción"
- "No puedes ocupar casillas conjuntas"
- "Tiene que ser un dado de..."
- Explaining mechanics or costs

**Map/terrain descriptions:**
- "Hazte cuenta que..." (imagine that...)
- "Es como una calle amplia..."
- Describing distances in pies or casillas

### When Voicing NPCs

The DM shifts voice depending on which NPC speaks. Key patterns:

| NPC | Voice Pattern |
|-----|---------------|
| **Donner IcePeak** | Military, tactical, commanding. Gives strategic assessments. Uses blunt soldier talk. |
| **Messi** (dwarf archer) | Practical, equipment-focused. Talks about ranged weapons, distances, positioning. |
| **Cory** (dwarf tank) | Short phrases, action-oriented. Charges into things. |
| **Achawn / Cham** (specialist) | Explosives talk, distraction tactics, bomb mechanics. |
| **Bixira** (rebel commander) | Urgent, commanding. Issues orders. "No me importa qué tengan que hacer. Lleguen." |
| **Deities (Val, Malfas)** | Cryptic, dramatic, archaic. Short impactful sentences. "Aún no es momento." |
| **Trolls / monsters** | The DM describes their actions rather than speaking for them. |
| **Robinson** | Enthusiastic, supportive. Beatbox references. "He soñado con esto toda mi vida." |

## General Attribution Heuristics

### Turn Structure
A typical turn follows this pattern:
1. **[DM]** "[Character name] qué quieres hacer?" — turn prompt
2. **[PLAYER]** Declares action — everything until the DM responds
3. **[DM]** Dice request: "Lanza un dado de 20"
4. **[PLAYER or TABLE]** Dice result: "17" / "Mierda, un 3"
5. **[DM]** Consequence narration: "Ves como..." / "Le pegas de lleno..."

### Simultaneous Conversation Clues
When multiple people talk at once (common in the transcripts), look for:
- **Ability mentions** to identify the player
- **Location consistency** — if the scene is at the tower, only characters at the tower can act
- **Emotional tone** — dramatic = Nixira, chaotic = Zacarías, terse = Tidus
- **Question patterns** — "Puedo hacer X?" is a player asking the DM; "Ves como X" is the DM narrating

### Communication Stone Conversations
When characters use the piedrita comunicadora, the DM usually signals the transition:
- "Por la piedrita..." / "Por el comunicador..."
- The voice on the other end is a different PC from the one currently in the scene
- This is how Tidus communicates when separated from the group

### NPC vs DM Narration
- If the DM describes what an NPC does in third person ("Donner saca su espada"), tag as **[DM]**
- If the DM speaks AS the NPC in first person ("No crees que ya lo hemos intentado?"), tag as **[DM as NPC_NAME]**
- The switch is usually signaled by a preceding "[DM] te dice:" or context of conversation
